# Helios H7 Smart Thermostat — Installation and User Guide

**Model:** Helios H7
**Firmware:** 3.2 (April 2026)
**Document version:** 1.4

## 1. Safety information

Before installing the Helios H7, turn off power at the breaker panel for any HVAC equipment connected to the wiring you will be working with. Failure to do so may result in electric shock or damage to the device.

The Helios H7 requires a C-wire (common wire) for continuous power. If your existing wiring lacks a C-wire, use the included Power Extender Kit (PEK) at the furnace control board.

The H7 is designed for indoor use only. Do not install in attics, garages, or any location where temperatures fall outside the range -10°C to 50°C (14°F to 122°F).

## 2. What's in the box

- Helios H7 thermostat unit
- Wall mounting plate
- Four #6 screws and four drywall anchors
- Power Extender Kit (PEK)
- Quick-start card (English and Spanish)
- USB-C cable (for firmware updates only — not for power)

## 3. Compatibility

The Helios H7 supports:

- Single-stage and two-stage heating
- Single-stage and two-stage cooling
- Heat pump systems with auxiliary heat
- Up to 2 heating stages + 2 cooling stages
- 24 VAC systems (standard residential)

The H7 does **not** support:

- High-voltage line systems (120 V or 240 V baseboards)
- Millivolt systems
- Steam heating systems
- Proprietary communicating systems (Carrier Infinity, Trane ComfortLink)

## 4. Installation

### 4.1 Remove the old thermostat

1. Turn off power at the breaker.
2. Pull the old thermostat off its wall plate.
3. Photograph the wiring before disconnecting any wires.
4. Label each wire by its terminal letter (R, C, W, Y, G, etc.).
5. Disconnect the wires and remove the old wall plate.

### 4.2 Install the Helios H7 wall plate

1. Hold the wall plate against the wall.
2. Mark and drill pilot holes for the four screws.
3. Use the included drywall anchors if not mounting into a stud.
4. Pull the wires through the opening in the wall plate.
5. Connect each wire to its corresponding labeled terminal.

### 4.3 Attach the H7 unit

1. Align the H7 unit with the wall plate.
2. Push gently until you hear a click.
3. Turn the power back on at the breaker.
4. The H7 boots and shows a "Welcome" screen within 30 seconds.

## 5. Wi-Fi setup

The H7 supports 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi (802.11 b/g/n). It does **not** support 5 GHz Wi-Fi or Wi-Fi 6.

To connect:

1. Open the Helios app on a phone connected to your home Wi-Fi.
2. Tap "Add device" and select "Helios H7".
3. Follow the in-app prompts.
4. Enter your Wi-Fi password when requested.
5. The H7 will reboot once and reconnect to your network.

## 6. Energy report

The H7 generates a monthly energy report visible in the Helios app and emailed to the address linked to your account. The report includes:

- Total runtime in hours, broken down by heating and cooling
- Estimated energy use in kWh
- Comparison to homes in the same ZIP code (anonymized)
- A "savings score" from 0 to 100

The savings score is computed from your runtime versus a regional baseline. A score of 80 or above indicates above-average efficiency for your area.

## 7. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Screen is blank | No C-wire power | Install PEK at the furnace |
| Wi-Fi keeps disconnecting | 5 GHz network selected by mistake | Reconnect to the 2.4 GHz SSID |
| HVAC won't respond | Mis-wired terminal | Re-check labels against installation photo |
| App can't find the H7 | H7 not in pairing mode | Hold the power button for 10 seconds to enter pairing mode |
| Temperature reading off by >2°C | Direct sunlight on unit | Relocate to a shaded wall |

## 8. Warranty

The Helios H7 is covered by a 3-year limited warranty against manufacturing defects. The warranty does not cover damage from improper installation, water damage, or use outside the specified temperature range.

To file a warranty claim, contact support@helios-example.com with your serial number and proof of purchase.
