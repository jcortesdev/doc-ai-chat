# Northwind Logistics — Q1 2026 Financial Summary

**Reporting period:** January 1, 2026 to March 31, 2026
**Document version:** Final, May 2, 2026
**Audit status:** Reviewed by Greenfield & Associates

## Executive summary

Northwind Logistics closed Q1 2026 with consolidated revenue of $148.2 million, a 12.4% increase year-over-year. Operating income reached $19.7 million, up from $14.1 million in Q1 2025, driven primarily by the Pacific Northwest expansion and the closure of two underperforming distribution centers in the Midwest. Free cash flow of $11.4 million returned the company to positive cash generation after a negative quarter in Q4 2025.

## 1. Revenue by segment

| Segment | Q1 2026 ($M) | Q1 2025 ($M) | YoY % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domestic freight | 82.5 | 76.3 | +8.1% |
| International freight | 31.9 | 24.6 | +29.7% |
| Warehousing | 21.8 | 22.4 | -2.7% |
| Last-mile delivery | 12.0 | 8.6 | +39.5% |
| **Total** | **148.2** | **131.9** | **+12.4%** |

International freight growth was driven by new contracts with two large e-commerce retailers in the European Union, ratified in late Q4 2025. Last-mile delivery grew on the back of the urban delivery hub launch in Seattle and Portland.

Warehousing revenue declined 2.7% due to the planned closure of the Indianapolis distribution center on February 14, 2026.

## 2. Operating expenses

Total operating expenses were $128.5 million, up 9.1% year-over-year. The largest cost categories:

- Driver wages and benefits: $52.1 million
- Fuel: $18.3 million
- Equipment depreciation: $14.7 million
- Facility lease: $12.2 million
- Insurance: $7.4 million

Fuel costs declined as a percentage of revenue from 13.8% to 12.3%, reflecting both lower diesel prices and improved route optimization through the deployment of the FleetSmart platform in 87% of long-haul vehicles.

## 3. Operating income and margin

Operating income reached $19.7 million, representing an operating margin of 13.3%. This is the highest quarterly operating margin reported by Northwind since Q2 2023.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Operating income ($M) | 19.7 | 14.1 |
| Operating margin (%) | 13.3 | 10.7 |
| Net income ($M) | 14.2 | 9.6 |
| Earnings per share ($) | 1.84 | 1.27 |

## 4. Cash flow and balance sheet

- Cash and equivalents at March 31, 2026: $86.1 million
- Free cash flow for Q1 2026: $11.4 million
- Total long-term debt: $142.3 million, down from $151.8 million at year-end 2025

The reduction in long-term debt reflects the early repayment of a $9.5 million term loan in March 2026, using proceeds from the sale of the Indianapolis facility.

## 5. Forward guidance

For full-year 2026 the company maintains its earlier guidance of:

- Revenue: $610-$640 million
- Operating margin: 11-13%
- Free cash flow: $42-$50 million

The company notes that ongoing uncertainty in fuel pricing and labor markets may affect operating margin in the second half of the year.

## 6. Notable events during the quarter

- January 9: New EU contract with Veloria Group, estimated annual value $14.5 million.
- February 14: Closure of Indianapolis distribution center, eliminating 86 positions. Severance and transition costs of $1.6 million recognized in Q1.
- March 28: Seattle urban delivery hub opened; eight new electric delivery vans deployed.
